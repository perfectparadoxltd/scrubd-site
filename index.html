// Questions
What file formats does it clean?

Images (JPEG, PNG, HEIC, TIFF and more), PDFs, Microsoft Office documents (.docx, .xlsx, .pptx) and video (MP4 and MOV). Scrubd is built to cover the files you actually share — not just photos. You can drag in a single file or a whole batch at once, and it handles each one with the right rules for its type.

Does anything get uploaded to the internet?

No. Auditing, stripping and writing clean copies all run entirely on your Mac. Your documents are never uploaded anywhere — there is no account, no cloud, no server that ever sees your files. The only network connections Scrubd makes are an update check and a one-off licence validation, never your files. You can confirm this with a firewall tool like Little Snitch or LuLu.

Does it overwrite my original, or make a copy?

By default Scrubd leaves your originals untouched and writes a clean copy alongside them (for example, report-clean.pdf), so you always have the source if you need it. If you'd rather clean in place, you can switch to overwrite — but the safe, copy-first behaviour is the default precisely so nothing is ever lost.

Does it really work with Word, Excel and PowerPoint?

Yes — that's the whole point. Office files quietly carry author names, your company name, tracked-change history, comments and revision data that survive normal sharing and that free EXIF tools simply don't touch. Scrubd opens the document package, strips those fields, and writes a clean file — in batch, so a folder of contracts is cleaned in one go.

What is an intent profile?

Instead of toggling dozens of individual fields, you pick the situation. "Publish to web" strips location and identity but keeps a file web-ready; "Send to client/lawyer" does an Office-grade scrub of authorship and history; "Strip everything" removes every removable field across every format. The audit view always shows you exactly what each profile will remove before you commit.

Who is Scrubd for?

Anyone who emails documents and would rather not hand over hidden data with them: lawyers and firms sending contracts, journalists protecting sources, freelancers sending deliverables to clients, and anyone publishing photos who doesn't want their home address baked into the GPS. If a file leaves your hands, Scrubd makes sure only the file leaves.

Is it notarized / safe to run?

Yes. Scrubd is sold directly and distributed as a Developer ID app, notarized by Apple. That keeps it lightweight while still being Gatekeeper-checked and safe to run. It needs no special system permissions beyond access to the files you drag in.

Is it a one-time purchase? How many Macs?

One-time, $14.99 — buy once, own forever, with free updates to your version. No subscription. A single licence covers all the Macs you personally own, with no per-seat counting.
